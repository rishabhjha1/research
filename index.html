Research Projects

  • Application of Explainable AI for Cardio Vascular Disease Research

    Explainable AI (XAI) is transforming cardiovascular disease research by enhancing the interpretability of complex machine learning models used in diagnosis and prognosis. XAI provides transparency by elucidating how models identify risk factors, predict outcomes, and recommend treatments. This fosters trust among clinicians and patients while enabling actionable insights. In cardiovascular research, XAI bridges the gap between advanced AI techniques and practical, human-centric medical decision-making, ensuring equitable and ethical healthcare advancements.

  • Brain Tumor Image Segmentation Using UNet

    **Brain Tumor Image Segmentation Using UNet** This analysis focuses on leveraging the UNet architecture for precise segmentation of brain tumors, specifically High-Grade Gliomas (HGG), using the BraTS dataset. UNet, a deep learning model designed for biomedical image segmentation, enables efficient identification of tumor boundaries, enhancing diagnostic accuracy. The study involves preprocessing MRI images, training the model on annotated data, and evaluating its performance using metrics like Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC). The results demonstrate the effectiveness of UNet in capturing complex tumor structures, providing a reliable tool for clinical applications and further research in automated tumor analysis.
